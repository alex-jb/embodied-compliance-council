## Project Summary (150 words max)

The Embodied Compliance Council is a research initiative producing a publicly-available, open-source AI deliberation infrastructure for regulated workflows. The system convenes five disagreement-by-design AI voices around each consequential decision (trading book changes in finance, loan and wealth-advisory decisions in banking), records each voice's verdict with confidence, and runs continuous Brier-score calibration to learn which voices' verdicts are load-bearing over time. A spatial extended-reality layer renders the council as a physical podium of voices, with cryptographic hash-chain audit of every adjudication. The project produces (1) a peer-reviewed publication at ICAIF 2027 on calibration discipline as audit primitive, (2) an IEEE banking paper on ECOA / Reg B disparate-impact detection via multi-voice review, and (3) an external NSF grant application building on Year-1 results. Two Katz graduate students participate as research assistants on banking risk math and spatial frontend implementation.
